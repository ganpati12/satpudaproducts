Sorghum popularly known as jowar is the most important food and fodder crop of dry land agriculture. The cereal crop is perennial in nature and possessing corn like leaves and bearing the grain in a compact cluster.Jowar is one of the major staple food grain crops in India. Out of the total area under jowar cultivation in India, 50% is cultivated in Maharashtra. Whereas out of the total production of Jowar in the nation, 52% is from Maharashtra. Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu.Jowar is commonly used in India as making bhakari.It is also consumed as popped jowar,jowar papad,cookies and other preparation.Flour is used for making unleavened flat bread,pancake dosa,porridge,delicious upma.In industry also it is used for alcohol preparation,jaggery,syrup,spirit,starch.
